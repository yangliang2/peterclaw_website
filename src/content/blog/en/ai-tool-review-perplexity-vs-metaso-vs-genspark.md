>
> When AI search engines evolve from "novelty toys" into "daily research infrastructure," choosing the right one means saving 30 minutes of information-sifting time every day.

## Review Background

In 2026, AI search engines have fundamentally changed how we acquire information. Google's "ten blue links" are being replaced by "direct answers + traceable citations." In this space, Perplexity, MetaSo (秘塔 AI 搜索), and Genspark are the three most discussed tools among Chinese users — yet their product philosophies couldn't be more different.

- **Perplexity** → The global leader in "conversational research engines," known for precise citations and multi-model switching
- **MetaSo** → The domestic "structured knowledge engine," breaking through with mind maps, academic search, and a completely free strategy
- **Genspark** → An "AI Agent workspace" evolved from search, distinguished by multi-modal deliverables and task automation

Comparisons in Chinese communities have remained at the "feature overview" or "personal preference" level, lacking horizontal comparisons in real research scenarios. This review aims to fill that gap.

This review is based on the following environment:

- **Test Period**: 2026-05-12 to 2026-05-26 (two weeks)
- **Test Scenarios**:
  1. Technical research: Investigating "AI Agent framework landscape in 2026," comparing search depth and information density
  2. Academic paper retrieval: Finding Chinese and English papers on "RAG system evaluation methodologies"
  3. Competitive analysis: Analyzing market positioning and pricing strategies of the three AI search tools
  4. Daily information verification: Processing 10+ daily queries across each tool over 5 days
- **System Environment**: macOS 15.4, Chrome 136, iPhone 15 Pro
- **Usage Pattern**: Each scenario was fully executed in all three tools
- **Subscription Status**: Perplexity Pro ($20/month), MetaSo (free), Genspark Plus ($24.99/month), all paid/used personally with no conflicts of interest

Target audience: Knowledge workers, developers, content creators, and researchers who use search engines daily for work information.

## Review Dimensions

This review focuses on five core dimensions:

1. **Search Quality & Information Density** — Accuracy, comprehensiveness, and clarity of information organization

## Perplexity: Overview

Perplexity is an AI search engine launched in 2022 by former OpenAI and Meta researchers. It pioneered the "conversational search" category: users ask in natural language, and AI returns a summarized answer with citations rather than a list of links. In 2026, Perplexity has over 100 million monthly active users and is the benchmark product in the global AI search space.

### Pros

**The citation system is an industry benchmark.** Every Perplexity answer includes numbered citations that link directly to original sources. In technical research on "best AI Agent frameworks in 2026," Perplexity provided 8 citations covering GitHub repos, tech blogs, official docs, and academic papers. Most impressive is the "Premium Sources" feature (Pro tier) — direct access to content from normally paywalled databases like Statista and PitchBook, which is unique among competitors.

**Multi-model switching is a researcher's dream.** Perplexity Pro allows free switching between GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet 4.6, Claude Opus 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Perplexity Sonar, and more. Testing revealed: Claude excels at deep analysis, Gemini at multi-modal tasks, and Sonar at real-time information retrieval. This ability to "choose the best brain for each question" gives Perplexity a clear advantage in complex research tasks over single-model competitors.

**The Comet browser redefines the search experience.** Perplexity's Comet browser integrates AI search directly into browsing — select any text on a webpage to ask follow-ups, compare perspectives across pages, and auto-generate reading summaries. In competitive analysis scenarios, Comet's "browse and ask" mode is 2–3× more efficient than the traditional "open search page → type question → wait for answer" workflow.

**Pro Search deep reasoning is outstanding.** For complex questions, Perplexity's Pro Search automatically decomposes into sub-questions, performs multi-round retrieval, and cross-validates. Testing "RAG evaluation methodologies," Pro Search first retrieved RAG basics, then separately retrieved evaluation metrics (Recall, Precision, MRR), mainstream benchmarks (RGB, AIRC, CRUD), and latest papers, finally synthesizing a well-structured research note.

### Cons

**Chinese content coverage has blind spots.** While Perplexity supports Chinese queries, its coverage of native Chinese sources — tech blogs, WeChat articles, Zhihu columns — is clearly weaker than MetaSo. Testing "latest updates on China's AI large model filing list," Perplexity's answer mainly cited English media and official announcements, lacking depth in Chinese discussions.

**The free tier is relatively restrictive.** Free users get only 5 Pro Searches per day. Standard searches are unlimited but use a lightweight model, so answer quality drops for complex questions. For heavy users with 10+ daily searches, the free tier is insufficient.

**Pricing is high for Asian users.** At $20/month (≈ ¥145) for Pro, plus currency fluctuation risks, the subscription is a significant expense for individual users in China. Max at $200/month exceeds most personal budgets.

**Privacy setting transparency could be improved.** Perplexity defaults to recording search history to "improve service." While this can be disabled in settings, the opt-out path is buried. Users handling sensitive information need to pay extra attention to this default data collection behavior.

## MetaSo (秘塔 AI 搜索): Overview

MetaSo (秘塔 AI 搜索) is a domestic AI search engine developed by Shanghai MetaSo Network Technology, launched in early 2024. Built on the self-developed MetaLLM large language model, it emphasizes an "ad-free, structured, traceable" search experience. As of 2026, MetaSo has surpassed 10 million monthly active users and is the most popular AI search engine in China, often called the "Chinese Perplexity."

### Pros

**Chinese content coverage is unmatched.** MetaSo's index is deeply optimized for the Chinese internet, with dramatically better coverage of WeChat articles, Zhihu, CSDN, Juejin, Jianshu, Bilibili subtitles, and other native sources than Perplexity. Testing "Vue 3 ecosystem best practices in 2026," MetaSo cited 6 Chinese tech blogs and 2 Juejin articles, while Perplexity found only 2 Chinese sources. For Chinese knowledge workers, this "local depth" is a decisive advantage.

**Structured output is the biggest highlight.** After every search, MetaSo auto-generates mind maps, outlines, timelines, and tables of related people/organizations/events. Researching "AI Agent framework evolution," the timeline view automatically mapped key milestones from 2023–2026, while the mind map helped quickly understand relationships between AutoGPT, LangChain, CrewAI, and other tools. This "one-click visualization" reduces information-organizing time from 30 minutes to 3 minutes.

**Completely free, with no feature restrictions.** MetaSo's Quick, In-Depth, and Academic search modes are all free. Deep Research offers 4–5 free uses daily, generating structured reports with reasoning chains and evidence tracing. Academic mode searches hundreds of millions of papers, supporting both Chinese and English academic search. Compared to Perplexity's $20/month Pro requirement for deep research, MetaSo's "free strategy" is extremely attractive for budget-sensitive users.

**Academic search is the strongest domestically.** MetaSo's Academic mode covers not only PubMed, arXiv, IEEE, and other international databases, but also deeply integrates CNKI, Wanfang, and VIP Chinese databases. Testing "RAG system evaluation methodologies," MetaSo found 12 relevant Chinese papers (including 3 published in 2026), while Perplexity returned only 5 English papers. For researchers needing Chinese literature, MetaSo is almost the only choice.

**Export and sharing experience is excellent.** Search results support one-click export to Word, PDF, or direct copy as Markdown. Mind maps can be exported as images or interactive links. In team collaboration, this "search equals report" experience is highly practical.

### Cons

**English content and international coverage lag.** While MetaSo supports English search, its coverage of English technical docs, Hacker News discussions, and Reddit communities is clearly weaker than Perplexity. For users who need to study both Chinese and English content, MetaSo may not fully replace Perplexity.

**Deep Research response time is long.** Each Deep Research takes 6–10 minutes. While result quality is high, urgent queries can't get instant answers. Perplexity's Pro Search usually completes in under 30 seconds; Genspark's Sparkpage delivers in under a minute.

**Free Deep Research quotas are limited.** At 4–5 uses per day, light users are fine, but those who need multiple deep investigations daily (investment analysts, consultants) will hit the cap quickly. Currently MetaSo has no paid expansion option, which is a pain point.

**No file upload or multi-modal analysis.** Perplexity Pro supports uploading PDFs, images, and audio for analysis; Genspark supports even more modalities. MetaSo currently doesn't support file uploads, meaning you can't directly "ask" a paper or a chart.

**Mobile experience lags behind desktop.** MetaSo currently only has a mobile web version, no standalone app. On iPhone, the interface adapts adequately, but mind map interactions are less smooth on small screens.

## Genspark: Overview

Genspark was founded by former Microsoft Bing and Baidu executives. It started as a "Sparkpage" search engine in 2024, then fully pivoted to an "AI Agent workspace" in 2025. It is no longer satisfied with "answering questions" — it directly "completes tasks," from search to generating slides, websites, videos, and data analysis reports. Genspark's Super Agent aims to replace multiple standalone tools. As of 2026, Genspark is valued at $1.6 billion with over 5 million monthly active users.

### Pros

**The search-to-delivery closed loop is unique.** Genspark's Super Agent can transform a single search request into multiple deliverables: research paper → slides, competitive analysis → data table, travel plan → itinerary website. Testing "AI Agent framework landscape research," Genspark not only provided a cited summary but also auto-generated a 12-page interactive Sparkpage, a comparison table, and an infographic. This "search equals production" model is extremely efficient in content creation and business presentation scenarios.

**Multi-modal output capability is the strongest.** Genspark supports generating text, slides (AI Slides), data tables (AI Sheets), images (FLUX, Ideogram), videos (Kling, Sora 2), and simple websites. In competitive analysis, I asked Genspark to "compare Perplexity, MetaSo, and Genspark pricing strategies and generate a slide deck for a team meeting" — 5 minutes later, I had an 8-page beautifully designed deck with comparison tables and trend charts.

**Mixture-of-Agents architecture delivers higher quality.** Genspark's backend isn't a single model but a hybrid architecture coordinating 9+ large language models and 80+ specialized tools. Claude handles planning and reasoning, GPT-4 handles deep research, Gemini handles multi-modal understanding. Testing showed that Genspark's complex question answering quality is indeed higher than single-model solutions — especially for cross-domain knowledge tasks.

**Relatively friendly for domestic access.** Genspark's server deployment is relatively friendly to Asian users, with stable access speeds from China. Combined with domestic APIs for GPT or Claude, latency is within acceptable ranges. For users in China with unstable network environments, Genspark's accessibility is better than Perplexity.

### Cons

**Pricing is high with complex billing.** Genspark Plus is $24.99/month ($19.99/month annually), $5 more than Perplexity Pro. More critically, the credit system — while chat is unlimited, generating slides, videos, and deep research tasks consume credits. During testing, generating 3 slide decks + 2 deep research tasks + 1 video consumed about 2,000 credits (Plus offers 10,000–12,000 monthly). For high-frequency users, monthly costs may approach $40–50.

**Search purity is diluted.** Genspark's core is "Agent workspace" rather than "search engine." If you just want to quickly verify a fact or find a code snippet, Genspark's interface feels overly complex — you need to choose among output types, wait for the Agent to plan, rather than getting an answer in seconds like Perplexity.

**Chinese content coverage lags behind MetaSo.** Genspark's index leans toward the English internet, with limited coverage of Chinese native sources. In Chinese technical query tests, Genspark often cited English docs and Wikipedia, while MetaSo could find the latest Chinese blogs and forum discussions.

**Citation system credibility is questionable.** While Genspark Sparkpages cite sources, testing revealed occasional "source-content mismatch" — a description of Perplexity pricing cited a link that actually pointed to a completely different article. This "hallucinated citation" is unacceptable in high-stakes research.

**Free tier is restrictive.** Genspark's free tier offers only 100–200 credits daily, quickly exhausted by multi-modal generation tasks. While basic search is free, Genspark's core selling points (Super Agent, slide generation, video generation) are limited in the free tier.

## Scenario-Based Recommendations

### If you are a developer or researcher who handles mixed Chinese-English information daily

**Recommended combo: MetaSo + Perplexity Pro.**

Use MetaSo for Chinese queries (deep coverage of tech blogs, academic papers, industry discussions) and Perplexity for English queries and international information (multi-model switching, Premium Sources, Comet browser). This combo costs $20/month but covers 95% of research scenarios. MetaSo's Academic mode + Perplexity's Pro Search is the most efficient dual-engine research configuration in 2026.

### If you are a content creator, influencer, or marketer who needs to quickly produce reports and presentations

**Recommend Genspark Plus.**

Genspark's "search → slides/tables/videos" closed loop accelerates content production. After researching a topic, directly generate deliverables for WeChat articles, Bilibili video scripts, or team meetings, eliminating time spent switching between multiple tools. At $25/month, if it saves you 5 hours monthly on layout and design, the ROI is positive.

### If you are a budget-sensitive student or freelancer

**Recommend MetaSo AI Search.**

MetaSo is completely free, and core features (three search modes, Deep Research, Academic Search, mind maps) are not restricted. Compared to Perplexity at $20/month and Genspark at $25/month, MetaSo is the best "zero-cost for 80% capability" option. The only compromises are English content coverage and file upload functionality.

### If you are an enterprise user focused on data compliance and privacy

**Recommend MetaSo (domestic business) or Perplexity Enterprise (international business).**

MetaSo stores data on domestic servers, complying with Chinese data regulations, and offers enterprise knowledge base API (¥0.03/query). Perplexity Enterprise Pro provides SSO, SCIM, data isolation, and admin controls, suitable for teams with international operations needing enterprise-grade security. Genspark's enterprise features are currently relatively thin.

### If you are an investment analyst, consultant, or strategy researcher who needs high-frequency deep research

**Recommend Perplexity Pro or Max.**

Perplexity's Pro Search deep reasoning and Premium Sources (Statista, PitchBook, CB Insights) are killer features for business research. Max's Model Council (three-model parallel validation) helps stress-test conclusions for high-stakes decisions. At $20–200/month, for professionals billing $100+/hour, breaking even requires saving just 2 hours monthly.

### If you primarily use search on mobile

**Recommend Perplexity (iOS/Android App) or MetaSo (mobile web).**

Perplexity's mobile app offers the best experience, with voice input, history sync, and bookmark management. MetaSo's mobile web is fastest to access from China but lacks a standalone app. Genspark's mobile experience is currently relatively weak, better suited for heavy desktop use.

## Final Verdict

> **Overall Recommendation: ★★★★★**
>
> Perplexity is the optimal choice for "research depth," MetaSo is the national favorite for "Chinese coverage + zero cost," and Genspark is the innovative experiment for "search to delivery." The three tools are not zero-sum competitors but complementary research infrastructure that can be combined.

**One-sentence summary**:

- If you pursue "the most rigorous citations + multi-model deep research" → **Perplexity Pro**
- If you pursue "Chinese local depth + zero cost + academic search" → **MetaSo AI Search**
- If you pursue "direct content/slide/video production from search" → **Genspark Plus**
- If you pursue "highest cost-performance across all scenarios" → **MetaSo (daily) + Perplexity Pro (deep research)**

**Future Watch Points**:

- Perplexity is strengthening its Chinese content indexing; H2 may narrow the gap with MetaSo in Chinese coverage
- MetaSo is rumored to launch a paid tier to unlock more Deep Research uses and file upload features; if pricing is reasonable, it may further consolidate domestic leadership
- Genspark is rapidly iterating citation system accuracy; solving "hallucinated citations" would significantly boost its research credibility
- All three tools are exploring "Agent proactively monitoring information sources and pushing summaries" — future AI search may shift from "you actively search" to "AI actively pushes"
